Day-1 Activities

Tiger Hill, famous for its panoramic sunrise views over the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas, offers a breathtaking sight that attracts visitors from far and wide, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and photographers.

Ghoom Monastery, also known as Yogachoeling Gompa, is a serene Buddhist monastery adorned with colorful prayer flags and a majestic statue of Maitreya Buddha, offering spiritual solace amidst the tranquil hills of Darjeeling.

Darjeeling Mall Road, bustling with shops selling local handicrafts, souvenirs, and delicious street food, is the vibrant heart of the town, where visitors can soak in the lively atmosphere and enjoy leisurely walks while taking in views of the surrounding mountains.

Day-2 Activities

Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its charming toy train rides through picturesque landscapes and quaint hill stations, offers a nostalgic journey back in time, providing a unique way to experience the beauty of the Himalayas.

Himalayan Mountaineering Institute, founded by Tenzing Norgay and Sir Edmund Hillary, offers insights into the world of mountaineering through its museum, training courses, and exhibits, making it a mecca for adventure enthusiasts and aspiring climbers.

Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park, home to rare Himalayan wildlife such as red pandas, snow leopards, and Himalayan black bears, provides a unique opportunity for visitors to observe and learn about these endangered species in their natural habitats.

Day-3 Activities

Japanese Peace Pagoda, a symbol of peace and harmony, offers a serene retreat with its tranquil surroundings and panoramic views of the Himalayas, providing a perfect spot for meditation and contemplation.

Darjeeling Ropeway, offering breathtaking aerial views of the town, tea gardens, and the distant Kanchenjunga peak, provides an exhilarating experience for visitors seeking adventure and stunning vistas.

Tibetan Refugee Self-Help Center, established to support Tibetan refugees, showcases traditional Tibetan handicrafts such as carpets, woodwork, and thangka paintings, providing visitors with a glimpse into Tibetan culture and craftsmanship while supporting a worthy cause.
