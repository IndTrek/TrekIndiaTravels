Day-1 Activities

Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, stands as a symbol of Mughal grandeur with its impressive red sandstone walls and intricate marble palaces, serving as a reminder of India's rich historical and cultural heritage.

Jama Masjid, one of the largest mosques in India, boasts stunning Mughal architecture with its towering minarets and marble domes, offering a spiritual retreat amidst the bustling streets of Old Delhi.

Chandni Chowk, a bustling market dating back to the Mughal era, entices visitors with its narrow lanes filled with shops selling everything from spices and fabrics to street food delicacies, providing a vibrant glimpse into Delhi's vibrant culture and commerce.

Day-2 Activities

Humayun's Tomb, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcases exquisite Mughal architecture and lush gardens, serving as a precursor to the Taj Mahal and offering a serene escape from the city's hustle and bustle.

Qutub Minarh, the tallest brick minaret in the world, is an architectural marvel dating back to the 12th century, surrounded by ancient ruins and intricate carvings, making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts and architecture lovers.

India Gate, a war memorial honoring Indian soldiers who sacrificed their lives in World War I, stands as an iconic symbol of national pride and unity, offering a picturesque setting for picnics and evening strolls.

Day-3 Activities

Akshardham Temple, a masterpiece of modern Indian architecture, showcases intricately carved sandstone and marble, depicting Indian culture, spirituality, and history, providing visitors with a spiritual and cultural journey through exhibitions, gardens, and performances.

Lotus Temple, renowned for its striking lotus-shaped architecture and serene ambiance, welcomes people of all faiths to meditate and pray in its tranquil surroundings, promoting peace and unity among all religions.

National Museum, the largest museum in India, houses a vast collection of artifacts spanning thousands of years of Indian history and culture, including sculptures, paintings, and archaeological treasures, offering a comprehensive insight into the country's rich heritage and artistic legacy.
