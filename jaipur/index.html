Day-1 Activities

Amber Fort, a majestic blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture, overlooks the scenic Maota Lake and offers a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the Rajput rulers, making it a must-visit destination in Jaipur.

City Palace, an exquisite blend of Mughal and Rajput styles, houses museums, courtyards, and gardens showcasing Jaipur's rich history and royal heritage, providing visitors with a fascinating journey through the city's past.

Jantar Mantar, an astronomical observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II, features a collection of architectural astronomical instruments that accurately measure time, predict eclipses, and track celestial bodies, making it a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a marvel of scientific innovation.

Day-2 Activities

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is an iconic Jaipur landmark with its unique facade of 953 windows, designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals while remaining unseen, offering a striking example of Rajput architecture and engineering.

The Albert Hall Museum, located in Jaipur, India, is a majestic 19th-century building that showcases a rich collection of historical artifacts, including costumes, paintings, and sculptures. Originally built as a town hall, it was later transformed into a museum, becoming a cultural gem and a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

Johari Bazaar, in Jaipur is a bustling marketplace renowned for its exquisite collection of traditional Rajasthani jewelry, gemstones, and textiles. Visitors can immerse themselves in the vibrant atmosphere, filled with colorful displays and the aroma of spices, making it a quintessential destination for shopping and cultural exploration in the Pink City.

Day-3 Activities

Jaigarh Fort, known as the "Fort of Victory," houses the world's largest cannon on wheels, Jaivana, and offers stunning views of the Amber Fort and the Jaipur cityscape, providing visitors with a glimpse into Rajasthan's martial history and military prowess.

Nahargarh Fort,, perched on the Aravalli hills, offers panoramic views of Jaipur city and surrounding landscapes, along with architectural marvels like the Madhavendra Bhawan, making it a popular destination for history buffs and photographers.

Jaipur's street, shopping scene offers a delightful blend of traditional crafts, vibrant textiles, and unique artifacts, making it a paradise for bargain hunters and culture enthusiasts alike. From bustling bazaars like Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar to charming alleys filled with artisanal treasures, Jaipur's streets beckon travelers to explore and discover the essence of Rajasthan's rich heritage.
